3. Demographic and Anthropometric data

For this project, Client refers to stroke patients typically age 40–80 undergoing lower-limb rehabilitation. The term physiotherapist is used broadly to include all rehabilitation specialists, such as occupational therapists and physiotherapists.

3.1 Obtaining Lower Limb Dimensions

To determine the frame dimensions, key anthropometric parameters of Singaporean adult males and females were referenced from Lee et al. (2019). Lower-limb length was estimated using segments 18 and 22, while foot dimensions were derived from segments 35 and 36 all of which are shown in the illustration below.

We then tabulated the combined anthropometric data corresponding to measurements in centimetres for both male and female participants containing the Combined Mean with ± 3 Standard Deviation, accommodating about 99.7% of Singapore’s adult population under a normal distribution (sample size ≈ 100).

In addition, we also obtained date from a nationally representative data from the Centre for Ageing Research and Education (CARES) at Duke-NUS. These data was derived from the PHASE study of Singaporeans aged 60 and above with cerebrovascular disease (e.g. Stroke), which provided popliteal height measurements corresponding to segment number 18 in the table above. Combining these with Lee et al. (2019) extends the range to better represent the local population including the elderly.

3.2 ROM

In order to provide a purposeful and comprehensive post stroke rehabilitation, exercises should first restore autonomy in activities of daily living (ADL) before targeting full motion.
