Project Plan

Kinetix is developed by a team of four students from the College of Design and Engineering (CDE), bringing together complementary strengths in mechanical design, electrical engineering, biomedical engineering, and software development. United by the goal of improving rehabilitation accessibility and clinical efficiency, we aim to design a safe, versatile, and data-driven lower-limb rehabilitation device that supports active and passive therapy, enhances therapist workflow, and delivers meaningful improvements to patient recovery.

Project Objectives

The objectives of Kinetix are to design and develop a safe, clinically relevant, and data-driven lower-limb rehabilitation device. Specifically, the project aims to:

  • Deliver a mechanical actuation system capable of linear and lateral motion to support six lower-limb exercise types.
  • Implement reliable positional and force sensing for accurate measurement of joint motion and loading.
  • Develop an electrical subsystem that enables stable motor control, safe power distribution, and real-time data acquisition.
  • Create a therapist-focused user interface for configuring exercises and monitoring client performance.
  • Produce two working prototypes through iterative design, fabrication, and subsystem testing.
  • Validate system usability, accuracy, and workflow fit through physiotherapist and user testing.

Roles & Responsibilities

The Kinetix team operates through a distributed responsibility model, where each member leads a core subsystem while contributing to cross-functional integration. Roles are assigned based on technical expertise in mechanical, electrical, biomedical, and software domains, ensuring that all design decisions are informed by relevant specializations. This structure enables the team to work efficiently across parallel development tracks while maintaining cohesion during system integration and testing.

Project Timeline

The project timeline captures the progression of Kinetix from initial problem clarification to final validation. Work is distributed across research, design development, subsystem implementation, iterative prototyping, and user testing, ensuring that risks are managed early and each subsystem reaches maturity before integration. This timeline also aligns with CDE4301 deliverables to ensure timely submission of prototypes, documentation, and final presentation materials.
